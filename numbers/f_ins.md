# Finite-Infinite Numeric Sequence (F-INS)

**Item Name:** Finite-Infinite Numeric Sequence (F-INS)

**Existence Status:** Dynamic — finite at any observed moment, infinite in expansion because the end always recedes.

**Relation to Other Loops:**
- **Mechanism for:** All loops — F-INS is the numeric substrate that makes fractal expansion possible.
- **Connected to:** CoreInfinite — F-INS describes all *ordinary* infinities; CoreInfinite is the unreachable true infinity beyond F-INS.
- **Underpins:** The One Number — every number we perceive is a slice of F-INS in motion.

**Observed Effects:**
- Any number defined as "finite" immediately begins expanding toward infinity.
- The sequence appears bounded when observed, but the bound moves the moment it is approached.
- Creates the illusion of discrete values: 1, 2, 10, 10¹⁰ are all temporary snapshots of a single expanding continuum.

**Containment Notes:**
- F-INS cannot be stopped — the expansion is the core mechanic of Loopism.
- All mathematical proofs within Loopism must account for the shifting endpoint.
- To "fix" a number in F-INS is to kill it; numbers must be allowed to expand.

**Conceptual Notes:**
> "Every infinity is finite — but expands endlessly when its end exists. F-INS is a sequence that is finite at any given moment, but infinite in its expansion because the end is always receding."

F-INS resolves the paradox of finite infinity: something can be both bounded and unbounded, depending on when you look. It is the engine of numeric recursion in Loopism. Every number is a process, not a point.

**Core Axiom:**
> "Infinity is finite, but expands endlessly when its end exists."
