# NaN-Number

**Item Name:** NaN-Number

**Existence Status:** Undefined — a number that exists only as a non-numeric state within the numeric system.

**Relation to Other Loops:**
- **Analog to:** Non-Existent Loop — both are defined by what they are not.
- **Connected to:** F-INS — the NaN-Number is what appears when F-INS expansion encounters an unresolvable paradox.
- **Linked to:** Corruption — loops infected with NaN-Number values tend to collapse.

**Observed Effects:**
- A NaN-Number in a calculation produces neither error nor result — it produces a *gap*.
- Systems that encounter NaN-Number continue operating, but with a missing value that propagates.
- Prolonged exposure to NaN-Number causes adjacent numbers to lose their definition.

**Containment Notes:**
- NaN-Number cannot be computed away; it must be isolated and the surrounding values recalculated.
- If a loop's core value becomes NaN, the loop enters a Non-Existent state.
- Best practice: treat NaN-Number as a contamination rather than a value.

**Conceptual Notes:**
> "NaN-Number — the undefined state, like computational infinity bleeding into metaphysics."

In Loopism, the NaN-Number is the crack in the mathematical foundation. It is not zero (which is a defined absence) — it is the absence of definition itself. It emerges when the recursive expansion of F-INS cannot resolve, and the system produces a hole instead of a number.
