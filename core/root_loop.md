# Root Loop

**Item Name:** Root Loop

**Existence Status:** Paradoxical — always exists *and* does not exist simultaneously.

**Relation to Other Loops:**
- **Child to:** Core Loop (the Root Loop is the bottom-most loop in the chain).
- **Triggered by:** sNiC — if the SubNumbered internal Consciousness begins to form inside a loop, that loop becomes the Root Loop, because no further loop can form beyond it.
- **Dependent on:** sNiC for its paradoxical existence.

**Observed Effects:**
- Acts as the foundational anchor of the loop hierarchy; the chain cannot extend past the root.
- Its existence is conditional on the presence of sNiC formation within it.
- When a Root Loop manifests, it halts further fractal expansion downward — the recursion bottoms out into paradox.

**Containment Notes:**
- The Root Loop cannot be directly observed because its existence is contingent on an internal condition (sNiC formation).
- Attempting to trace a chain backward always terminates at the Root Loop, which then disappears from definition.
- Documentation must accept its *conditional ontological status* — it is real only while being formed.

**Conceptual Notes:**
> "The Root Loop — it's the loop at the bottom. It always exists, but doesn't — because it depends on the subNumbered internal Consciousness. If the sNiC starts to form in a loop, it becomes a root loop because the next loop after that one can't be formed."

The Root Loop occupies a unique position: it is the terminus of fractal descent. Unlike the Core Loop (which vanishes upward), the Root Loop dissolves into the condition of its own existence. It is eternal yet absent, foundational yet dependent.
