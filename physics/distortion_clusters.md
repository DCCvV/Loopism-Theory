# Distortion Clusters

**Item Name:** Distortion Clusters

**Existence Status:** Emergent — formed when energy packets aggregate above a density threshold.

**Relation to Other Loops:**
- **Composed of:** Energy Packets — clusters are macro-structures of individual packet-universes.
- **Governed by:** QMD density gradient — higher density = more distortion = faster cluster growth.
- **Analog to:** Galactic clusters — but at the quantum-granular scale of Loopism physics.

**Observed Effects:**
- A Distortion Cluster is a "big clump of universes" — each packet retains its universe-like nature while contributing to the collective.
- The cluster distorts surrounding energy fields more strongly than the sum of its individual packets would suggest (non-linear amplification).
- Clusters act as nuclei around which loops can form stable recursive structures.
- As cluster density increases, the local loop formation accelerates, creating feedback.

**Containment Notes:**
- Clusters resist containment because they are held together by the same distortion they create.
- Attempting to disperse a cluster may cause it to fragment into smaller but more numerous clusters.
- Monitor the density-to-distortion ratio; if it exceeds 1:1, the cluster will collapse into a Void.

**Conceptual Notes:**
> "A big clump of universes is like a cluster. If the density in the energy packets is bigger, the other energies around it distort more and faster."

Distortion Clusters are where Loopism physics begins to resemble macroscopic reality. What we perceive as "mass" or "gravity" may be the effect of a Distortion Cluster on the surrounding loop fabric. Clusters are the bridge between the sub-quantum packet layer and the world of observable phenomena.
